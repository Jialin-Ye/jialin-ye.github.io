Publications

  • 2025.02.25
    Hierarchical Behavioral Analysis Framework (HBAF) as a platform for standardized quantitative identification of behaviors
    Cell Reports
    In this paper, we propose a framework for the standardized and quantitative identification of behaviors across multiple hierarchical levels, termed the Hierarchical Behavioral Analysis Framework (HBAF). Using HBAF, we investigate the spontaneous behavioral patterns of mice under various experimental conditions, including acute stress models, different times of day, light exposure, and sex-related differences.
  • 2024.06.20
    Mapping the behavioral signatures of Shank3b mice in both sexes
    Neuroscience Bulletin
    Autism spectrum disorders (ASD) are neurodevelopmental disorders, and mutations in the Shank3b gene represent one of the most widely studied mouse models for ASD research. It is well established that Shank3b knockout (Shank3b -/-) mice exhibit core symptoms such as social deficits and repetitive, stereotyped behaviors. However, the spontaneous behavioral repertoire of these mice remains incompletely characterized. In this study, we employed a 3D behavioral analysis system to investigate the spontaneous behavioral features of male and female mice across three genotypes—wild-type, heterozygous (Shank3b +/-), and homozygous (Shank3b -/-)—within the same litter.
  • 2022.10.14
    Lifespan changes in cannabinoid 1 receptor mRNA expression in the female C57BL/6J mouse brain
    Journal of Comparative Neurology
    This study systematically mapped age-related changes in CB1 receptor (CB1R) mRNA expression across 36 brain regions in female mice, revealing region-specific trajectories—including cortical increases and subcortical decreases—highlighting the importance of age and sex as critical factors in endocannabinoid signaling.
  • 2022.09.05
    Connectivity map of subthalamic corticotropin-releasing hormone neurons in the mouse brain
    Neuroscience Bulletin
    In this study, the intricate neuroanatomy of CRH cell types in the mSTN with critical functions in the integration of stress responses and sleep/wake states is identified for the first time. The structural basis we provide helps to better understand non-motor STN functions and also offers a potential target for the treatment of sleep disturbance-related psychiatric disorders.
  • 2022.04.06
    The subthalamic corticotropin-releasing hormone neurons mediate adaptive REM sleep responses to threat
    Neuron
    Sleep and predator defense are both essential instinctive behaviors for individual survival, yet they inherently present a degree of conflict. This study is the first to reveal a shared neural circuit that regulates both rapid eye movement (REM) sleep and innate fear responses. The emergence of this energy-efficient brain mechanism may have evolved and been reinforced through adaptation to environmental threats. The discovery of this shared circuitry suggests that REM sleep may play a previously underappreciated role in predator defense. This finding provides a potential evolutionary explanation for the well-documented clinical co-occurrence of increased REM sleep and stress-related emotional disorders.
